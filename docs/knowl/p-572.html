Whenever confronted with a logical implication of the form \(\mathcal{P}\implies\mathcal{Q}\text{,}\) where \(\mathcal{P}\) and \(\mathcal{Q}\) denote arbitrary propositions, you should always ask whether the implication “goes the other way”. In other words, does the reverse implication \(\mathcal{Q}\implies \mathcal{P} \) also hold?
