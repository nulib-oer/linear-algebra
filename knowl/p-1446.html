Assume \(\Span S=V\text{.}\) Let \(S'\) be a subset of \(S\) of minimal cardinality such that \(\Span S'\) is still equal to \(V\text{.}\) Such a set is guaranteed to exist: since \(S\) is finite, it has a finite number of subsets; of these subsets some will span, others will not; of the subsets that do span, there will be one (or more) that has the least number of elements.
