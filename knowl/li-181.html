Item 4.

The matrix \(A= \begin{amatrix}[rrr] 1\amp 1 \amp 1\\ 1\amp 1 \amp 1\\ 1\amp 1\amp 1 \end{amatrix}\) is not invertible. To see why we make use of the row method of matrix multiplication.

Indeed, given any matrix \(B\text{,}\) each row of \(AB\) is given by \(\begin{amatrix}1\amp 1\amp 1 \end{amatrix}\,B \text{.}\) It follows that all the rows of \(AB\) are identical, and hence that we cannot have \(AB=I_3\text{,}\) since the rows of \(I_3\) are not identical.
