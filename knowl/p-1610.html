\begin{equation*} a(x^2+x+1)+b(x^2-x)+c(x^2-1)=\boldzero \end{equation*}
corresponds to a solution \(\boldx=(a,b,c)\) to the matrix equation \(A\boldx=\boldzero\text{.}\) Since \(A\) is invertible, we conclude that \(\boldx=\boldzero\) (TheoremÂ 2.5.27), and thus that \(a=b=c=0\text{.}\) This shows \(B\) is linearly independent.
