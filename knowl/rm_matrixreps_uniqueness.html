Remark 5.2.7. Uniqueness of \([T]_B^{B'}\).

The uniqueness property described in (2) of TheoremÂ 5.2.6 provides an alternative way of computing \([T]_{B}^{B'}\) that can be useful in certain situations: namely, simply provide an \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\) that satisfies the defining property
\begin{equation*} A[\boldv]_B=[T(\boldv)]_{B'} \end{equation*}
for all \(\boldv\in V\text{.}\) Since there is only one such matrix, we must have \(A=[T]_B^{B'}\) in this case.
