Proof

Suppose \(A\) is invertible with inverse \(A^{-1}\text{.}\) The theorem claims \(A^T\) is invertible, and that in fact \((A^T)^{-1}=(A^{-1})^T\text{.}\) To prove this, we need only show that

\begin{equation*} A^T(A^{-1})^T)=(A^{-1})^T)A^T=I\text{.} \end{equation*}

We verify the two equalities separately:

\begin{align*} A^T(A^{-1})^T\amp =\left(A^{-1}A\right)^T \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_trans_props.html}{\text{Theorem 3.2.11}})\\ \amp =I_n^T \\ \amp =I_n \checkmark \end{align*}
\begin{align*} (A^{-1})^TA^T \amp =(AA^{-1})^T \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_trans_props.html}{\text{Theorem 3.2.11}})\\ \amp =I_n^T=I_n \ \checkmark\text{.} \end{align*}

In both chains of equality we make use of the obvious claim \(I^T=I\text{.}\)
