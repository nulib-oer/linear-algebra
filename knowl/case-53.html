Case: \(i=j\).

In this case we have

\begin{align*} [A\, \adj A]_{ii} \amp =\sum_{k=1}^na_{ik}[\adj A]_{ki} \\ \amp =\sum_{k=1}^n a_{ik}((-1)^{i+k}M_{ik}) \amp (\knowl{./knowl/d_adjoint.html}{\text{Definition 3.5.14}} )\\ \amp =\sum_{k=1}^n(-1)^{i+k}a_{ik}M_{ik} \\ \amp =\det A \text{.} \end{align*}

A similar argument shows that \((\adj A\, A)_{ii}=\det A\text{,}\) though in this case we use expansion along a column.
