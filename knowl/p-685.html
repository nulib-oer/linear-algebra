We give a proof only for the the first equation: \(AX=Y\text{.}\) To show the equivalence we must show the two implications \(AX=Y\implies X=A^{-1}Y\) and \(X=A^{-1}Y\implies AX=Y\text{.}\) To this end, we have
\begin{align*} AX=Y \amp\implies A^{-1}AX=A^{-1}Y \\ \amp\implies IX=A^{-1}Y \\ \amp\implies X=A^{-1}Y \text{,} \end{align*}
and
\begin{align*} X=A^{-1}Y \amp\implies AX=AA^{-1}Y \\ \amp\implies AX=IY \\ \amp\implies AX=Y \text{.} \end{align*}
