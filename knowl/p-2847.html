Given \(n\)-tuples \(\boldx=(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\) and \(\boldy=(y_1,y_2,\dots, y_n)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} \boldx\cdot \boldy \amp = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \amp x_2\amp \dots \amp x_n\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y_1\\ y_2\\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{bmatrix} \\ \amp=\boldx^T\boldy \text{,} \end{align*}
where in the last equality \(\boldx\) and \(\boldy\) are treated as column vectors.
