Theorem 5.4.25. Characteristic polynomial.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix.
  1. The function \(p(t)=\det (tI-A)\) is a monic polynomial of degree \(n\) with real coefficients: i.e.,
    \begin{equation} p(t)=t^n+a_{n-1}t^{n-1}+\cdots +a_1t+a_0, a_i\in \R\text{.}\tag{5.4.6} \end{equation}
  2. Over the complex numbers \(\C\) we can factor \(p(t)\) completely as
    \begin{equation*} p(t)=(t-\lambda_1)(t-\lambda_2)\cdots (t-\lambda_n), \end{equation*}
    where the \(\lambda_i\in \C\) are the (not necessarily distinct) roots of \(p(t)\text{.}\)
  3. The eigenvalues of \(A\) are precisely the real roots of \(p(t)\text{:}\) i.e., the \(\lambda_i\) for which \(\lambda_i\in \R\text{.}\)
  4. The coefficient \(a_{n-1}\) in (5.4.6) satisfies
    \begin{equation} a_{n-1}=-\tr A=-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2+\cdots +\lambda_n)\text{.}\tag{5.4.7} \end{equation}
  5. The coefficient \(a_0\) in (5.4.6) satisfies
    \begin{equation} a_0=(-1)^n\det A=(-1)^n\lambda_1\lambda_2\cdots \lambda_n\text{.}\tag{5.4.8} \end{equation}
  6. We conclude that
    \begin{align} \tr A \amp = \lambda_1+\lambda_2+\cdots +\lambda_n \tag{5.4.9}\\ \det A\amp = \lambda_1\lambda_2\cdots \lambda_n \text{.}\tag{5.4.10} \end{align}

Proof.
