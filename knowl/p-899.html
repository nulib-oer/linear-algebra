  1. If A has dimensions \(m \times n\) and B has dimensions \(n \times r\text{,}\) then AB has dimensions \(m \times r\text{.}\)
  2. If A has dimensions \(5 \times 4\) and B has dimensions \(4 \times 3\text{,}\) then the 3rd row, 4th column entry of AB is obtained by multiplying the 3rd column of A by the 4th row of B.
