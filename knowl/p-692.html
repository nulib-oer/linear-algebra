The matrix \(A= \begin{amatrix}[rrr] 1\amp 1 \amp 1\\ 1\amp 1 \amp 1\\ 1\amp 1\amp 1 \end{amatrix}\) is not invertible. Indeed, using the row method of matrix multiplication, we see that given any matrix \(B\text{,}\) each row of \(AB\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \begin{amatrix}1\amp 1\amp 1 \end{amatrix}\,B \text{.} \end{equation*}
It follows that all the rows of \(AB\) are identical, and hence that we cannot have \(AB=I_3\text{,}\) since the rows of \(I_3\) are not identical.
