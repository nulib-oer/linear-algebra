Item 4.
A polynomial on \(X\) of degree at most \(n\) is a polynomial of the form \(f(x)=\anpoly\text{,}\) where \(a_i\in \R\text{.}\) (See Section 0.7, and in particular Definition 0.7.1 for more details about polynomials.) Recall that if \(a_n\ne 0\text{,}\) we call \(n\) the the degree of \(f\text{,}\) denoted \(\deg f\text{.}\)
The set of polynomials of degree at most \(n\) on \(X\) is denoted \(P_n(X)\text{;}\) the set of all polynomials on \(X\) is denoted \(P(X)\text{.}\) When \(X=\R\text{,}\) we shorten the notation to \(P_n\) and \(P\text{.}\)
