The projection \(\hat{\boldy}\) makes the error \(\norm{\boldy-\hat{\boldy}}=\sqrt{ \epsilon_1^2+\epsilon_2^2+\epsilon_3^2}\) as small as possible. This means if I draw any other line and compute the corresponding differences \(\epsilon_i'\) at the \(x\)-values -3, 1 and 2, then
\begin{equation*} \epsilon_1^2+\epsilon_2^2+\epsilon_3^2\leq (\epsilon_1')^2+(\epsilon_2')^2+(\epsilon_3')^2 \end{equation*}
