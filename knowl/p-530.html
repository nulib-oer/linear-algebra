The formula for the \(ij\)-th entry of a matrix product \(AB\) can be succinctly described as the dot product of the \(i\)-th row of \(A\) with the \(j\)-th column of \(B\text{.}\) You may have already met the dot product in the special case of \(2\)- and \(3\)-tuples; the definition generalizes easily to \(n\)-tuples for any positive integer \(n\text{.}\) We will have a lot more to say about the dot product and related operations in ChapterÂ 4. For now we will provide an official definition so that we can conveniently describe matrix multiplication in terms of dot products.
