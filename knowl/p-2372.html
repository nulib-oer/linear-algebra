Let \(B\) and \(B'\) be two ordered bases of the \(n\)-dimensional vector space \(V\text{.}\)
  1. Recall that \(\id_V\colon V\rightarrow V\) is the identity transformation (DefinitionÂ 3.2.3), defined as \(\id_V(\boldv)=\boldv\) for all \(\boldv\in V\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation*} \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}=[\id_V]_{B}^{B'}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. For all \(\boldv\in V\) we have
    \begin{equation} \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[\boldv]_B=[\boldv]_{B'}\text{.}\tag{5.3.1} \end{equation}
    In other words, to convert the \(B\)-coordinates of a vector \(\boldv\in V\) to \(B'\)-coordinates, simply multiply on the left by the matrix \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)
  3. Property (5.3.1) defines \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) uniquely: i.e., if \(A\) satisfies \(A[\boldv]_B=[\boldv]_{B'}\) for all \(\boldv\in \R^n\text{,}\) then \(A=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)
