Corollary 2.4.15. Row equivalence and invertible matrices.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be \(m\times n\) matrices. The following statements are equivalent.
  1. The matrices \(A\) and \(B\) are row equivalent.
  2. There are elementary matrices \(E_1, E_2, \dots, E_r\) such that \(E_rE_{r-1}\cdots E_1A=B \text{.}\)
  3. There is an invertible \(m\times m\) matrix \(Q\) such that \(QA=B\text{.}\)
