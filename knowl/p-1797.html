Let \(\boldv=(a_i)_{i=1}^\infty\) and \(\boldw=(b_i)_{i=1}^\infty\) be two infinite sequences in \(\R^\infty\text{.}\) For any \(c,d\in\R\) we have
\begin{align*} T_\ell(c\boldv+d\boldw) \amp=T_\ell\left((ca_i+db_i)_{i=1}^\infty \right)\amp (\knowl{./knowl/ex_vs_infinitesequences.html}{\text{Definition 3.1.7}}) \\ \amp= (ca_{i+1}+db_{i+1})_{i=1}^\infty \amp (\text{by def.})\\ \amp=c(a_{i+1})_{i=1}^\infty+d(b_{i+1})_{i=1}^\infty \amp (\knowl{./knowl/ex_vs_infinitesequences.html}{\text{Definition 3.1.7}})\\ \amp=cT_\ell(\boldv)+dT_\ell(\boldw)\amp (\text{by def.}) \text{.} \end{align*}
This proves \(T_\ell\) is a linear transformation.
