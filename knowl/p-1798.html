To verify the rank-nullity theorem, we must compute bases for \(\NS T\) and \(\im T\text{.}\) Consider first \(\NS T\text{.}\) We have
\begin{align*} \NS T \amp =\{(x,y,z)\colon x+y+z=0\}\\ \amp =\{(-s-t,s,t)\colon s,t\in\R\} \text{.} \end{align*}
Here the parametric description is obtained using our usual technique for solving systems of equations (ProcedureÂ 1.3.5). From the parametric description, it is clear that the set \(B=\{(-1,1,0), (-1,0,1)\}\) spans \(\NS T\text{.}\) Since \(B\) is clearly linearly independent, it is a basis for \(\NS T\text{,}\) and we conclude that \(\dim \NS=\val{B}=2\text{.}\) (Alternatively, the equation \(x+y+z=0\) defines a plane passing through the origin in \(\R^3\text{,}\) and we know such subspaces are of dimension two. )
