Theorem 5.4.14. Eigenvectors of matrices.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix. Fix \(\lambda\in \R\text{.}\)
  1. The eigenvectors of \(A\) with eigenvalue \(\lambda\) are the nonzero elements of the subspace \(\NS(\lambda I-A)\text{.}\)
  2. As a consequence, \(\lambda\in \R\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\) if and only if the matrix \(\lambda I-A\) is singular (i.e., not invertible).
