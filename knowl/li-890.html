Item 2.
The orthogonal set \(S\) is linearly independent by Theorem 4.2.2. Let \(S=\{\boldv_1,\boldv_2,\dots, \boldv_r\}\) be the distinct elements of \(S\text{.}\) (We must have \(r\leq n\) since \(S\) is linearly independent.) By Theorem 3.7.8 we can extend \(S\) to a basis \(B=\{\boldv_1,\dots, \boldv_r, \boldv_{r+1}, \dots, \boldv_n\}\text{.}\) It is easy to see that when we apply the Gram-Schmidt procedure to \(B\text{,}\) the first \(r\) vectors are left unchanged, as they are already pairwise orthogonal. Thus Gram-Schmidt returns an orthogonal basis of the form \(B'=\{\boldv_1,\dots, \boldv_r, \boldw_{r+1}, \dots, \boldw_n\}\text{,}\) as desired.
