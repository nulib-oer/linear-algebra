Extending to a basis
Assume \(S\) is linearly independent. To extend \(S\) to a basis of \(V\) proceed as follows.
  1. Pick any ordered basis \(B\) of \(V\) and let \(S'=\{[\boldv_1]_B, [\boldv_2]_B, \dots, [\boldv_r]_B\}\subseteq \R^n\text{.}\)
  2. Use the relevant procedure of Procedure 3.8.13 to extend \(S'\) to a basis \(T'=\{[\boldv_1]_B, [\boldv_2]_B, \dots, [\boldv_r]_B, \boldx_{r+1}, \dots, \boldx_n \}\) of \(\R^n\text{.}\)
  3. By (3) of Theorem 5.1.12, for all \(r+1\leq i\leq n\) there are vectors \(\boldv_{i}\in V\) satisfying \([\boldv_i]_B=\boldx_i\text{.}\) The set \(T=\{\boldv_1,\dots, \boldv_r,\boldv_{r+1},\dots,\boldv_n\}\) is a basis of \(V\) containing \(S\text{.}\)
