If the product of two matrices is the zero matrix, we cannot conclude that one of matrices is the zero matrix. In logical notation:
\begin{equation} \underset{m\times n}{A}\, \underset{n\times r}{B}=\boldzero_{m\times r}\;\not\!\!\!\!\implies A=\boldzero_{m\times n} \text{ or } B=\boldzero_{n\times r}\text{.}\tag{2.2.1} \end{equation}
