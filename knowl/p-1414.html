Given vectors \(\boldv, \boldv'\in V\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} T(\boldv)=T(\boldv') \iff \boldv'=\boldv+\boldu \text{ for some } \boldu\in \NS T\text{.}\tag{3.4.2} \end{equation}
Equivalently, given \(\boldw\in W\) and \(\boldv\in V\) satisfying \(T(\boldv)=\boldw\text{,}\) the set \(X\) of all \(\boldv'\) satisfying \(T(\boldv')=\boldw\) can be described as
\begin{equation} X=\{\boldv+\boldu\colon \boldu\in \NS T\}\text{.}\tag{3.4.3} \end{equation}
